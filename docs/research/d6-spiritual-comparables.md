# D6 — Spiritual / Holistic / Alternative-Wellness Comparable Organizations

> **Key Stat Callout**
> The most defensible insight from this research: *no existing organization combines licensed psychedelic-assisted facilitation, breathwork, and somatic practice with a peer-led professional men's network in a single ongoing membership container.* Every org profiled below occupies at most two of those three nodes. The gap is real, structural, and currently unoccupied in South Florida.

---

## Comparable Organizations Profiled

### 1. Conscious Capitalism, Inc. (CCI)

**Member archetype.** CEOs, founders, presidents, and C-suite executives of purpose-driven companies. The annual CEO Summit is invite-only. Chapter members skew toward entrepreneurs running companies of 10–500 employees; also attracts investors, impact-sector attorneys, sustainability consultants, and B-Corp-aligned business owners. Politically and spiritually ecumenical — the framework is secular-ethical, not spiritual. [T2: consciouscapitalism.org membership page; T2: consciouscapitalismfl.org]

**Acquisition model.** Chapter events, referral from existing members, EO/YPO crossover, press coverage of "purpose-driven business" trend. CCI has ~70 active U.S. chapters and several international. [T2: consciouscapitalism.org]

**Retention dynamics.** The Four Tenets framework (Higher Purpose, Stakeholder Orientation, Conscious Leadership, Conscious Culture) gives members a shared language that sustains engagement. Retention risk: members who resolve the philosophical tension between profit and purpose may feel they've "graduated." The framework is intellectually engaging but light on lived ritual or embodied practice — nothing bonds at the nervous-system level. [T2: consciouscapitalism.org; T3: EO Network blog post on Four Tenets, eonetwork.org]

**Pricing.** Chapters set their own dues; publicly available data is sparse. The Dallas chapter lists tiered pricing; other chapters do not publish rates. The Florida chapter (consciouscapitalismfl.org) lists no membership fees as of 2025. The national CEO Summit is a separate paid event. [T2: consciouscapitalismdallas.org/Membership; T4: no statewide public pricing confirmed]

**Geographic footprint.** Conscious Capitalism Florida chapter is active; Tampa Bay appears to be the most active node. Miami/South Florida presence is minimal — the CCF Partner Program ran a single Miami workshop (date unconfirmed in public record). No dedicated South Florida chapter has been identified. [T2: consciouscapitalism.org/story/ccf-partner-program-miami; T2: consciouscapitalismfl.org]

**Ethos / language.** "Elevating humanity through business." "Higher purpose beyond profit." "Conscious leader." The tone is HBR-adjacent: aspirational but secular. Spirituality is implicit at best. The word "soul" does not appear in official CCI materials; "purpose" appears dozens of times. [T2: consciouscapitalism.org]

### 2. ManKind Project (MKP) — New Warrior Training Adventure

**Member archetype.** Broad demographic range but preponderantly 35–55, college-educated, urban or suburban, with a meaningful subset of mental-health professionals, therapists, coaches, and clergy. A longitudinal study published in the *American Journal of Community Psychology* found significant pro-social behavioral changes at 1- and 2-year follow-up, suggesting the organization retains men who integrate the experience — which implies a self-selecting population already disposed to introspection. MKP has served over 75,000 men globally across 35+ years. [T1: published MKP longitudinal study, mankindproject.org/published-study; T2: mankindproject.org]

**Acquisition model.** Word of mouth is primary; the NWTA is described internally as something existing members "staff" or "bring men to." No paid advertising model is publicly documented. Media coverage (notably a 2024 *OutSmart Magazine* profile) and podcast appearances sustain awareness. [T2: mankindproject.org; T3: outsmartmagazine.com/2024/11/mankind-project-new-warrior-training]

**Retention dynamics.** Post-NWTA, men join ongoing peer-facilitated I-Groups (Integration Groups) — weekly circles of 8–12 men using MKP tools for conflict resolution, emotional intelligence, goal accountability. MKP Florida lists six regional communities including a Southeast Florida node. A 1990s D.C.-area study found 23 of 45 I-groups still active 8 years later — roughly 50% long-run survival [older]. The model is low-cost and peer-led, which reduces barriers but also reduces professional facilitation quality. [T2: florida.mkpusa.org/mens-groups; T3: MKP Primary Integration Training manual, mkpconnect.org]

**Pricing.** The NWTA costs $375–$1,195 depending on location and track (outdoor/reduced-amenity versions lower). Florida trainings (Silver Springs, FL) run twice yearly (March and November 2026 confirmed). [T2: mankindproject.org/new-warrior-training-adventure; T2: florida.mkpusa.org]

**Geographic footprint.** MKP Florida has six regions; "Southeast Florida" is named as one node. Active I-groups exist in the South Florida area. Silver Springs (Central Florida) is the primary NWTA venue for the state. [T2: florida.mkpusa.org]

**Ethos / language.** "Modern male initiation." "Emotionally mature, powerful, compassionate, and purpose-driven men." "Peer-facilitated men's groups." Language is psycho-therapeutic and masculinity-focused, drawing on Jungian archetypes [older]. Not explicitly spiritual but ritual and ceremony are embedded in the NWTA format. [T2: mankindproject.org/who-we-are]

### 3. Sacred Sons

**Member archetype.** Men aged roughly 27–50, disproportionately from Western spiritual communities — yoga practitioners, wellness entrepreneurs, coaches, creatives, and some therapists. The Convergence draws what a 2024 observer described as "one of the most diverse" men's work gatherings, with age range skewing late 20s to mid-40s. Sacred Sons has trained 500+ men as facilitators, suggesting a large practitioner-professional subset within the community. [T3: Ian MacKenzie, Medium, "Sacred Sons and the Rise of the Embodied Masculine"; T2: sacredsons.com]

**Acquisition model.** Instagram-led discovery; the organization deliberately broke with the secrecy norms of earlier men's movements to make vulnerability visible on social media. Founder content (Aubert Bastiat, pre-controversy) and facilitator-led promotion drove growth. [T3: MacKenzie, Medium; T2: sacredsons.com social channels]

**Retention dynamics.** The funnel is: social media content → free or low-cost Saturday online circle ($10–20 suggested donation) → Immersion (one-day, $299) → EMX (72-hour retreat, 30–60 men) → Leadership Pathway (10-week online + 4-day in-person threshold) → ongoing membership via The Circle (subscription). The multi-tier approach is designed for progressive deepening. Retention risk: significant 2024 governance crisis — co-founder Aubert Bastiat faced misconduct accusations; multiple core facilitators departed citing unresolved safety concerns. This is a material reputational liability for any organization benchmarking against Sacred Sons. [T3: MacKenzie 2024 author's note; T2: sacredsons.com/pricing-plans; T2: sacredsons.com/leadership-pathway]

**Pricing.** Saturday online circle: $10–20 donation. Immersion (one day): $299. EMX (72-hour retreat): pricing not publicly listed; scholarship program (1 in 5 spots) suggests $800–$1,500 range based on comparable market events. Leadership Training: $999 listed. The Circle (ongoing membership): pricing not confirmed in public sources. [T2: sacredsons.com/pricing-plans; T4: price range estimated from search results and comparable events]

**Geographic footprint.** National and international (Convergence X billed "Across Nations"). Florida EMX events are listed on their calendar. No permanent South Florida chapter structure — event-based only. [T2: sacredsons.com/product/emx-florida; T2: sacredsons.com/convergence-x]

**Ethos / language.** "Co-steward the return of the father archetype." "Brotherhood is the medicine." "Embodied practice, ceremony, drum-led journeys, honest relational work." Language blends Jungian masculine archetypes (Magician, Lover, Warrior) with somatic healing vocabulary. Sacred Sons explicitly incorporates plant medicine (Kambo), sweat lodge, breathwork, and tantric sexuality teachings — among the most spiritually forward of the orgs profiled here. [T3: frequencybeat.com/listing/sacred-sons; T3: MacKenzie, Medium]

### 4. EVRYMAN

**Member archetype.** Men 25–60 across a wide occupational range, with a notable concentration of high-performing executives and senior professionals. Fortune Well (2024) documented attendees including a managing director overseeing 150 employees at a multinational software company and a former NBA conditioning director. EVRYMAN's founder framing — "on paper, he had everything society said would make him happy" — resonates specifically with successful men experiencing meaning deficits. The tech/startup ecosystem has been an early adopter: the org attracted substantial media coverage from CNBC (2018) after gaining traction in tech circles. [T3: Fortune Well, 2024; T3: CNBC, 2018; T2: evryman.com/about]

**Acquisition model.** Retreat-to-circle pipeline: men attend a weekend retreat ($1,000–$2,500 market rate for peer retreats per Fortune Well), then join a local circle of 6–10 men for weekly 60–90-minute calls. Local chapters are member-organized. A significant media presence (Men's Health, CNN, CNBC, Fortune) has driven organic awareness. [T3: Fortune Well, 2024; T2: evryman.com/faq]

**Retention dynamics.** The weekly circle format creates habitual engagement. No subscription contract — pay-per-event model reduces friction to join and to leave. Major structural risk: by end of 2023, EVRYMAN's core leadership team had stepped away; Palm Venture Studio acquired the assets. The organization has scaled back programming and membership options. Co-founder Owen Marcus launched MELD (Men's Emotional Leadership Development) as a successor vehicle in 2024. [T3: owenmarcus.com/evryman; T2: evryman.com/faq]

**Pricing.** $30/month membership (as listed on evryman.com) for community platform access and weekly calls. Retreat pricing follows market rate ($1,000–$2,500 for weekend events). No South Florida chapter confirmed. [T2: evryman.com/faq; T3: Fortune Well, 2024]

**Geographic footprint.** National chapter model; chapters in major U.S. cities. No Miami/South Florida chapter confirmed in public data as of 2025. Men in underserved cities are encouraged to start chapters. [T2: evryman.com/faq]

**Ethos / language.** "Destigmatize men's vulnerability and emotionality." "Creating yourself, not finding yourself." "Relax, Open and Connect." The language is therapeutic-secular with somatic framing — no explicit spiritual vocabulary. Positioned as emotional intelligence training for men rather than spiritual community. [T3: Schlafman, Medium, "A Weekend in the Woods with EVRYMAN"; T2: evryman.com/about]

### 5. Authentic Relating (ART International)

**Member archetype.** Broad and deliberately inclusive — ART has delivered training to "spiritual seekers in Bali, jail inmates in Colorado, executives in New York City," across 50+ locations on four continents. The community skews urban, educated, and curious about interpersonal dynamics. A meaningful professional subset exists in coaching, therapy, facilitation, and HR. The practice is not men-specific; it is co-ed and orientation-agnostic. [T2: authenticrelating.co]

**Acquisition model.** Free community events (Games Nights) on Meetup.com and local chapters create a low-friction entry point. ART Community Circles are held weekly on Zoom, free and open. Progressive training model: Level 1 course → Level 2 → facilitator certification → coach certification. [T2: authenticrelating.co/art-community-circles; T2: authenticrelating.co/courses]

**Retention dynamics.** The free weekly circle is the retention anchor. The facilitator-certification pipeline converts engaged members into community leaders, which deepens investment. Attrition risk: because the practice is non-exclusive and the core techniques are freely available (the Games Manual is a public PDF), members who learn the skills may not feel ongoing organizational affiliation is necessary. [T2: authenticrelating.co; T3: authrev.org]

**Pricing.** Level 1 course pricing not publicly confirmed in indexed sources. Level 2 course: $365 early bird / $465 regular. Community circles: free. Level 3 (7-day relational leadership retreat): pricing not found. [T2: authenticrelating.co/courses/level-2]

**Geographic footprint.** International; dozens of local communities worldwide. No dedicated South Florida chapter found in public search. Meetup.com is the primary discovery mechanism for local groups; Miami presence is unconfirmed at the organized-chapter level. [T3: meetup.com/topics/relating-games]

**Ethos / language.** "The art of being human." "Fully, beautifully human." "No scripts, no pressure — just space." The language is relational-humanist, not spiritual. Closest to therapy-adjacent group-process work. No ritual, ceremony, or plant medicine content. [T2: authenticrelating.co/art-community-circles]

### 6. MAPS (Multidisciplinary Association for Psychedelic Studies) — Adjacent Professional Network

**Member archetype.** MAPS does not operate a membership network in the traditional sense — it is a nonprofit research and advocacy organization. Its adjacent professional community spans researchers, therapists, policy advocates, military/veteran advocates, entrepreneurs, and investors. Psychedelic Science 2023 (Denver, June 2023) drew 12,500 in-person attendees and 10,000+ virtual — described as the largest psychedelic conference in history. Documented attendee types: clinicians, legal professionals, indigenous leaders, policymakers (FDA representatives, state elected officials), Netflix filmmakers, and "people new to psychedelics." High-profile adjacent figures: Whole Foods co-founder John Mackey, NFL QB Aaron Rodgers, author Michael Pollan, mycologist Paul Stamets. [T2: maps.org; T3: lucid.news/maps-psychedelic-science-2023; T3: maps.org/2023/05/04/psychedelic-science-announces-community-partnerships]

**Acquisition model.** Annual flagship conference (Psychedelic Science, held approximately every 2 years) is the primary acquisition event. MAPS also produces an International Therapist Education Program for licensed clinicians. [T2: maps.org/evolving-education]

**Retention dynamics.** MAPS retains community through research publication, the conference series (Psychedelic Science 2025 announced for Denver, June 2025), and the Therapist Education Program. No ongoing peer-circle or local chapter model. [T2: maps.org/2024/02/13/psychedelic-science-2025-save-the-date]

**Pricing.** Conference tickets not confirmed in current indexed sources. Therapist Education Program pricing not publicly indexed. [T4: no confirmed current pricing]

**Geographic footprint.** Global research organization; no Florida-specific chapter or local community structure. Florida has individual psychedelic integration practitioners (Mindful Psychedelics, North Miami Beach; Love Discovery Institute, Coral Gables; Rising Heart Integrative Coaching, South Florida) but no organized MAPS-affiliated professional network locally. [T2: mindfulpsychedelics.co; T2: lovediscovery.org; T2: risingheartintegrativecoaching.com]

**Ethos / language.** "Legal, responsible, evidence-based pathways to psychedelics." "End psychedelic prohibition." Clinical-research frame. The language is rigorously scientific and advocacy-forward — not spiritual, not communal. [T2: maps.org]

### 7. Florida-Specific Psychedelic Integration and Breathwork Circles

**Organized community landscape.** Florida has a distributed ecosystem of integration communities, none of which has achieved the scale or professionalism of a national organization:

- **Psychedelic Society of Southwest Florida** (psychedelicswfl.org): Re-launched meetings Spring 2024. Focus on education, open dialogue, and community connection around psychedelics, mental wellness, and personal growth. Meets in-person; no membership pricing found. [T2: psychedelicswfl.org]
- **Tampa Bay Psychedelic Community** (Meetup): Free or low-cost educational workshops, integration circles, social events. [T3: meetup.com Tampa Bay Psychedelic Community]
- **Orlando Psychedelic Society** (Meetup): Mission-driven; emphasizes healing and spiritual awakening. [T3: meetup.com Orlando Psychedelic Society]
- **Cannadelic Global** (Miami): Annual conference (Miami Airport Convention Center, May 2025) framing itself as "the world's largest cannabis and psychedelics conference." Community of healers, seekers, psychonauts, wellness practitioners; events include sound healing, breathwork, somatic work, yoga, tantra, workshops. Rooted in Florida Cannabis Coalition, active since 2014. [T2: cannadelic.miami; T3: miaminewtimes.com/Cannadelic coverage]
- **Holotropic Breathwork Miami** (Jeffrey Warren): Residential retreats 35 minutes from Miami International Airport (Homestead, FL). 4-day format on a mango grove. Small-group, practitioner-facilitated. Not a membership community. [T2: jeffreycharleswarren.com/holotropicbreathwork-miami-retreat-2024]
- **Healing Men's Circle at Breathe Miami**: Meets at Aya Apothecary Spa, NW Miami. Event-based (October 2024 documented); "sacred masculinity," alchemical framing. [T3: breathemiami.us/events/healing-mens-circle]
- **Sacred Space Miami** (sacredspacemiami.com): Farm-anchored wellness center in Miami; spiritually aligned community space; no men's-specific circle identified. [T2: sacredspacemiami.com]

**Summary gap.** No South Florida organization combines licensed psychedelic facilitation, peer professional networking, and an ongoing membership structure. The scene is event-driven and practitioner-centric, not community-organized.

## Member-Archetype Profile That Fits the Tribe's Spiritual / Holistic Angle

Drawing across all organizations above and psychedelic-use research, the target member persona for the Lumenary Tribe is:

**"The Accomplished Seeker"**

- **Age:** 35–52
- **Professional identity:** Founder, CEO, partner, or senior executive — most commonly in tech, creative industries, integrative health, real estate, finance, or professional services. Has achieved conventional markers of success and is experiencing a meaning ceiling.
- **Psychographic:** High in the personality trait of Openness (confirmed correlate of both entrepreneurial success and psychedelic receptivity per *Journal of Psychopharmacology* research [T1: Erritzoe et al., 2019, journals.sagepub.com/doi/abs/10.1177/0269881119827891]). Likely has existing practices: meditation, yoga, cold plunge, breathwork. May have completed a retreat (ayahuasca, psilocybin, holotropic breathwork) and found the integration unsupported.
- **Relationship to psychedelics:** Not a novice seeker; more likely a practitioner seeking a peer container that matches his professional seriousness. Research on psychedelic-assisted therapy trials notes that RCT participants skew toward higher education levels — this is the same profile [T1: ScienceDirect systematic review of PAT trial diversity, 2025].
- **Social context:** Socially isolated at the peer level despite extensive professional networks. Data from EVRYMAN coverage: only 22% of men would turn to friends in a crisis [T3: Fortune Well, 2024]. BNI/EO connections feel transactional; this person is hungry for men who can be both business peers and genuine brotherhood.
- **Geographic signal in South Florida:** The Miami-Fort Lauderdale corridor has a high concentration of entrepreneurs, Latin American professional diaspora, and the financial/tech/creative convergence of Brickell-Wynwood-Design District. Cannadelic's annual Miami presence suggests an organized demand signal already exists.
- **Acquisition trigger:** He will not respond to "men's group." He will respond to "a professional network for men who take their inner life as seriously as their P&L."

## Positioning Gap the Tribe Can Own

Every organization profiled above has a ceiling it cannot pass through:

| Organization | What it does well | What it cannot offer |
|---|---|---|
| Conscious Capitalism | Professional peer network, purpose framing | No embodied practice, no ritual, no spiritual depth |
| ManKind Project | Deep initiation ritual, I-group retention | No professional overlay; light on alternative-wellness integration |
| Sacred Sons | Spiritual + somatic depth, plant medicine | Governance risk; no professional filter; event-based only |
| EVRYMAN | Emotional-intelligence bridge for exec men | Leadership collapse (2023–24); no spiritual or psychedelic frame |
| Authentic Relating | Relational skill-building, broad access | No professional filter, no ritual, no men-specific depth |
| MAPS / Psychedelic community | Cutting-edge psychedelic research community | No professional peer network; no ongoing circle container |

**The unoccupied position:** A vetted, ongoing professional men's network in South Florida that integrates:
1. Business-peer accountability (EO-style), with the credibility filter of professional achievement
2. Embodied and holistic practice (breathwork, somatic work) as a standing program element, not an annual retreat
3. Licensed, facilitated access to psychedelic-assisted experiences as a formal offering within the community — not a side referral

No existing organization in Florida or nationally holds all three nodes simultaneously inside a single ongoing membership container. The tribe can own the phrase: *"Where serious men do serious inner work — together."*

The South Florida market has unmet demand: Cannadelic fills the festival-seeker end; MKP fills the initiatory-weekend end; individual practitioners (Mindful Psychedelics, Love Discovery Institute) fill the clinical end. The middle — an ongoing professional peer circle with spiritual teeth — is genuinely vacant.
