# §1 — Executive Summary

The five findings that earn the engagement.

**1. The positioning gap is real, structural, and unoccupied.**
No organization in South Florida — and none nationally — currently combines
executive-grade peer accountability (EO/Vistage caliber), intentional men's
work (MKP/Sacred Sons depth), and integrated somatic, breathwork, and
embodied-practice programming inside a single ongoing membership container.
Every existing org owns one or two of those nodes and openly cedes the
third. The Tribe's defensible position: *"Where serious men do serious
inner work — together."*

**2. $1,200–$1,500/yr is a market-validated price band.**
BNI Miami-Dade charges $1,099–$1,398/yr (confirmed from official dues
page) and successfully enrolls thousands of South Florida professionals at
that level. ProVisors clears $1,800/yr for ~9,000 trusted advisors
nationwide. EO South Florida costs $6,371/yr. Vistage $10,500–$16,500.
Tiger 21 $33,000. The Tribe's current $750/yr is materially below the
proven market floor for a structured professional community in this
region — leaving $450–$750/yr per member on the table at current
scale alone.

**3. Brickell and Coral Gables are the obvious Year 1 chapters.**
Brickell ranks #1 in South Florida law-firm office demand (Feb 2026), holds
60+ international banks, posts a 3.7% submarket vacancy, and houses the
densest concentration of attorneys, hedge funds, and PE principals in
Miami-Dade. Coral Gables holds 4,114 professional services workers, $134K
median household income, and absorbs the Brickell overflow at half the rent
($54/SF vs. $108/SF). These two chapters alone, at $1,500/yr × 30 members,
generate $90,000 in annual dues — and serve as the proof-of-concept
for the rest of the rollout.

**4. Optimal chapter size is 25–35 active members; hard cap 40.**
This number is anchored on three independent lines of evidence:
Dunbar's friendship layer (~50 people you'd reconvene with after months
apart) sets the cognitive ceiling; BNI's global data identifies 30–40
members as the productivity peak; group-cohesion research shows meeting
quality degrades above 35 without sub-group structures. Chapter splits
are triggered at 38+, not as failure but as growth.

**5. The target member already exists in measurable density across S. FL.**
"The Accomplished Seeker" — 35–52, founder/CEO/partner-track, high in
Openness, with prior breathwork or psychedelic exposure but no peer
container that matches his professional seriousness — is a documented
demographic. Psychiatrists' therapeutic openness to psychedelics doubled
from 42% (2016) to 81% (2024); urban, educated, male, under-50 are the
strongest openness predictors; the Miami post-2020 founder migration
brought thousands from San Francisco and New York with this exact profile.

## Cohort Weighting Protocol — bias Founder selection toward 38–45

Within the Accomplished Seeker range, **weight the Year-1 Founders cohort
toward the younger end (38–45) to maximize cumulative chapter LTV,
network-building velocity, and engagement compounding.** Older qualified
prospects are not declined — they are slotted into Year-2+ growth seats
once chapter culture is established.

**The LTV math the founders will recognize:**

| Entry age | Avg tenure (ASAE peer-org benchmark) | Lifetime dues @ $1,500/yr |
|---|---|---|
| 38 | ~12 years | **~$18,000** |
| 45 | ~10 years | ~$15,000 |
| 52 | ~8 years | ~$12,000 |
| 58 | ~6 years | ~$9,000 |

A 30-seat chapter biased toward the younger half of the range vs. the
older half generates roughly **$270,000 in additional cumulative dues
over chapter lifetime** — before retreat add-ons, override extensions,
or referral revenue.

**Industry validation already in this binder:**
- **YPO caps membership at age 45** because they have measured that
  network-building velocity peaks in members' 30s–40s. *(D5)*
- **BNI documents 35–50 as the highest-engagement age band** for chapter
  participation and referral throughput. *(D2)*
- **Affinity-activity participation skews younger** (Strava run-club Gen Z
  data, Mindbody breathwork attendance trends). Younger Founders convert
  affinity-event attendance into membership at higher rates. *(L4)*

**The compounding effect:**
A 38-year-old Founder who stays 12 years becomes a *10-year evangelist* —
recruiting his peer cohort, sponsoring chapter splits, and sustaining
culture into Year-3. A 58-year-old Founder typically reduces participation
in years 4–6 (retirement, slower travel, lifestyle shift), forcing
seat-replacement work that drains chapter momentum.

**Operational rule for prospect intake:** when two qualified prospects
surface in the same seat category for the same chapter, default to the
younger one for Year-1 Founder selection. Document this as the *Cohort
Weighting Protocol* in the membership-committee playbook.

## Why this band is defensible

**Below ProVisors, above BNI — the structural sweet spot.**
BNI Miami-Dade charges $1,099 (renewal) to $1,398 (new member) and
delivers a referral network with no identity programming, no retreat,
no inner-work component. ProVisors charges ~$1,800/yr and offers a
trusted-advisor directory and monthly meetings — also without
transformational programming. The Tribe at $1,500–$1,800 sits **between
two proven price points in the same market**, while offering a layered
product neither competitor provides: business peer accountability +
spiritual/somatic integration + curated brotherhood.

**The "Under BNI" framing is factually accurate and emotionally usable.**
You can stand in front of the founders and say, with citations: *"BNI
charges $1,398 in Miami for new members to join a referral network. We
charge $1,500 for the same investment plus actual brotherhood, plus
infrastructure they don't have."* That sentence is bulletproof.

**At current $750/yr, every member represents $450–$1,050 in foregone
ARR.** A 200-member organization is leaving **$90,000–$210,000 of
recurring revenue** on the table per year, before accounting for retreat
and add-on revenue. This is the single most valuable insight you can
hand the founders in the first thirty minutes of the meeting.

**Comparison anchors the founders will recognize:**

| **BNI Miami-Dade** | **$1,099–$1,398** | Weekly referrals among non-competing pros |

**Path to $3,000–$5,000/yr by Year 3.** As the Tribe layers in psychedelic
integration programming, quarterly immersives, and proprietary retreat
infrastructure, the dues line graduates upward — not as a price hike,
but as a programming upgrade. EO and Vistage prove this market exists.
