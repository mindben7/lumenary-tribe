# Slice 1 — Founder Proposal Design

This document specifies the design of the deliverable Ben will hand to the
tribe founders to propose his engagement. It is not the proposal itself — the
proposal will be drafted from this spec by the implementation plan that
follows.

## 1. Purpose

Land a long-term, well-compensated engagement for Ben as the tribe's
**Fractional CMO + Production Principal + Miami Expansion Lead**, structured
in a way that:

- Survives review by veteran lawyers, CPAs, and commercial real estate
  operators without losing credibility.
- Aligns Ben's incentives with multi-year organizational growth, not just
  short-term content output.
- Builds in the Miami chapter leadership Ben has been verbally promised at
  month 6 with concrete economics.
- Creates IP and equity assets Ben owns that survive the engagement.
- Is explicitly defensible under non-profit governance rules (no equity in
  the parent non-profit; equity, if any, lives in a for-profit sister LLC).

## 2. Audience characterization (informs tone)

The founders are not typical SMB owners. They are:

- Lawyers, CPAs, and commercial real estate veterans
- People who read documents for a living and will mark up the term sheet
  before they discuss the narrative
- Skeptical of marketing pitches; respect numbers, term sheets, and structure
- Likely to test for scope creep and weak language
- AI-naïve relative to Ben — Ben's AI/production stack is the unique value

**Tone implications:** business-formal, declarative, numbers-forward, no
adjectives. Sentences that sound like LOI language, not agency copy. Honest
about the org's current state (audit before pitch).

## 3. Deliverable architecture: two artifacts

### Part A — Executive Term Sheet (2 pages)

The contract-shaped document the founders can hand to their attorney for
markup. Bullet format. Numbers, dates, percentages, terms. Reads like a
Letter of Intent.

Sections:
1. Parties (Ben's LLC ↔ the non-profit; sister LLC if applicable)
2. Engagement scope (one paragraph)
3. Term and termination
4. Compensation (base, override, bonuses, IP royalty, ad mgmt %, production
   passthrough)
5. Sister LLC equity (conditional)
6. Miami chapter leader provision
7. IP ownership and license
8. Non-compete / non-solicit
9. Confidentiality
10. Dispute resolution and governing law (Florida)
11. Signature block

### Part B — Engagement Proposal (10–14 pages)

The narrative + tables that justify the term sheet. Sections:

1. **Executive Summary** — half a page. What, why, what it costs them, what
   it earns them.
2. **Where the tribe is today** — the audit they didn't ask for. ARR estimate,
   chapter map, member acquisition reality, brand state, content state. Ben's
   competence proof.
3. **The 36-month thesis** — why this org can be 5–10× larger and what must
   be true for that to happen.
4. **Ben's role: Fractional CMO + Production Principal** — scope, decision
   rights, what he owns operationally, what he does not.
5. **Engagement structure** — Scenario 3 (Non-Profit Edition) spelled out
   with rationale for each lever.
6. **First 90 days: Discovery → Foundation** — week-by-week deliverables.
7. **Year-1 and Year-3 financial picture** — two tables: org's investment
   vs. expected revenue lift; Ben's earnings under conservative / base /
   aggressive growth.
8. **KPIs and how we measure them** — the dashboard Ben will be judged on.
9. **About Ben** — short. Production credentials, AI background, four
   meetings already filmed.
10. **Appendices** — content production cadence sample, ad strategy outline,
    BOS overview, KPI definitions, sample weekly cadence.

### Why two artifacts, not one deck

A deck forces a synchronous presentation. A term sheet lets sophisticated
readers reach yes/no/redline in 10 minutes — which is how lawyers prefer to
make decisions. The narrative becomes the leave-behind they read alone, when
their guard is down. When they redline, they redline the term sheet (not
the narrative), which keeps scope creep contained.

A pitch deck (Part C) is **not** included by default. Add only if the founders
explicitly request a presentation format.

## 4. Locked engagement structure — Scenario 3 (Non-Profit Edition)

| Lever | Term |
|---|---|
| Base retainer | $7,000/mo paid to Ben's LLC; 12-month initial term; 60-day mutual notice termination after initial term |
| Chapter override | 15% of dues from any chapter Ben personally launches, for 24 months from that chapter's launch date |
| IP royalty | License fee for Ben-owned BOS, content library, ads playbook, chapter-launch templates, recruiting curriculum. Recommended: $1,500/mo flat floor OR $5/active-member/mo, whichever is greater. Term: auto-renews annually unless terminated. |
| Performance bonuses | $1,500 per new chapter launched to capacity (~25 paying members within 6 months of launch). Capped at 6/year for IRS reasonableness. |
| Production passthrough | $3,000/mo, billed at cost with declared markup (typically 0–10%); Ben's LLC manages production team |
| Ad management | 8% of managed paid media spend (Meta, LinkedIn, Google) |
| Miami chapter leader (M6+) | $1,500/mo stipend per chapter Ben personally leads, OR 12% of that chapter's dues, whichever is greater |
| For-profit sister LLC equity | 10–15% founding operator equity in the for-profit sister LLC. Vest: 25% cliff at month 12, then monthly over 24 months. **Conditional** on sister LLC existing or being created (proposed in Phase 0). |
| Non-compete | 12 months post-engagement, Broward + Miami-Dade only, narrowly scoped to "for-profit men's networking organizations." Carve-outs: organization-sanctioned chapter leadership, Ben's other marketing/content/AI clients, non-competing community organizations. |
| Non-solicit | Standard 12-month non-solicit of active tribe members for competing-org purposes |
| IP ownership | Ben's LLC retains ownership of all software, frameworks, and templates created. Non-profit receives perpetual license tied to active engagement; license terminates if engagement ends without renewal. Content footage and edited reels: org has perpetual usage license; Ben retains portfolio rights. |

**Removed from earlier drafts:**
- ❌ Equity in the parent non-profit (impossible — no owners exist)
- ❌ Profit share from the non-profit (private inurement risk)
- ❌ $50/net-new-member bonus (too low to defend administering)
- ❌ N→S brokerage line ($250/conversion) — handled separately as a future
  Member Referral Marketplace (logged as Slice 2/3 backlog item)

## 5. Phase 0 — Discovery + Foundation (Months 1–2)

The first 8 weeks are scoped as **Discovery + Foundation Sprint** at the
standard $7K/mo retainer. Deliverables:

- Org audit: chapter count/map, member roster, dues collected, churn rate,
  brand inventory, current content output
- Confirmation of legal entity classification (501(c)(3) / (c)(6) / (c)(7))
  and existence/absence of for-profit sister LLC (resolves Open Items
  **B3** and **B4**)
- If no sister LLC exists: prepared recommendation memo for creating one,
  including governance, ownership structure, and Ben's founding equity
- Pricing analysis: defensible target pricing tier ($1,200–1,500/yr) with
  competitive context (BNI, ProVisors, EO chapter dues)
- Brand and positioning audit (current vs. competitive set)
- Content production system v0: cadence, asset pipeline, AI workflow
- Modernized website v0 design direction
- KPI dashboard design (the metrics Ben will be measured on)

Phase 0 ends with a Phase 1 kickoff document that confirms scope, pricing,
team additions, and ad budget for the operator phase.

## 6. Financial picture — three Miami growth scenarios

Assumptions:
- Org-wide post-increase dues: **$1,200/yr** per active member
- Existing Broward/N. Miami chapters continue unchanged (no override there)
- Ben earns override **only** on chapters he personally launches
- Ben earns chapter leader stipend **only** on chapters he personally leads
- IP royalty assumed to ramp from $1,500/mo floor toward $5/member/mo as
  total active members grow
- Ad spend grows from $3K/mo → $20K/mo over 36 months

| Scenario | Conservative | Base | Aggressive |
|---|---|---|---|
| Year 1 chapters launched | 1 | 2 | 3 |
| Year 2 chapters launched (cumulative) | 3 | 5 | 7 |
| Year 3 chapters launched (cumulative) | 5 | 8 | 12 |
| Avg members/chapter Y3 | 25 | 30 | 35 |
| Year 3 Miami ARR contribution | $150K | $288K | $504K |

**Ben's 36-month cumulative earnings (Aggressive scenario):**

| Stream | Approx. 36-month total |
|---|---|
| Base retainer ($7K × 36) | $252,000 |
| Chapter override (12 chapters, ramping cohort by cohort) | ~$110,000 |
| Miami chapter leader stipends (3–5 chapters led) | ~$60,000 |
| IP royalty (ramps $1,500 → $2,500/mo) | ~$45,000 |
| LLC distributions (Year 2+) | ~$25,000 |
| Performance bonuses | ~$25,000 |
| Ad management fees | ~$48,000 |
| **Aggressive case total** | **~$565,000** |

Approximately **$15,700/mo average** with the curve back-loaded to Year 3.
At $1,500/yr dues instead of $1,200, the aggressive case clears **~$700K**
over 36 months.

The Engagement Proposal (Part B §7) will present **all three** scenarios in
the same format so the founders can see Ben's earnings transparently
under each.

## 7. KPIs (the dashboard Ben proposes to be measured on)

Defined in the proposal, tracked in the BOS, reported quarterly:

- **Member growth:** net-new active members per quarter, by chapter
- **Chapter velocity:** chapters launched, time-to-capacity, churn-out
- **Member ARR:** total recurring dues collected
- **Cost per acquired member:** ad spend ÷ paying conversions
- **Content velocity:** reels/long-form/AI explainers shipped per month
- **Engagement:** WhatsApp activity, event attendance ratio, member NPS
- **Pipeline:** Chamber outreach → discovery call → applicant → active

## 10. Drafting principles for Part A and Part B

When the implementation plan produces the actual artifacts:

- **Numbers before adjectives.** Every claim of value must trace to a number
  or a defined deliverable.
- **Defensible language.** No "industry-leading," "world-class," "synergies."
  Read aloud — if it sounds like agency copy, rewrite.
- **Active voice, declarative.** Mirror the cadence of the LOIs and term
  sheets these founders read regularly.
- **Honest audit before pitch.** The audit section must call out at least
  three specific weaknesses in the org's current state. This is the
  trust-builder.
- **Footer disclaimer:** "This proposal contains business terms only.
  Definitive agreements subject to legal and tax review by counsel for
  both parties."
- **Define key terms inline.** Specifically: "active member" (paid-current
  AND attended at least one meeting in the trailing 60 days), "launched to
  capacity" (≥25 paying members within 6 months of first meeting), "managed
  ad spend" (paid media run by Ben on the org's behalf, excludes
  organic/owned channels). Lawyers will demand these definitions; embedding
  them up front prevents redlines.
